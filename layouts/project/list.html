01

Mission background

European defence forces require large numbers of expendable UAVs for rapid deployment scenarios. Current propulsion solutions are too costly, slow to manufacture, and insufficiently scalable to meet operational demand.

02

Critical gaps

Existing small turbojet engines rely on conventional subtractive manufacturing, limiting geometric complexity and production speed. Stealth compatibility and low-cost attritable design remain unresolved engineering challenges.

03

Technologies

Additive manufacturing (AM) is applied to key engine components — centrifugal compressor, combustor, turbine — to enable rapid batch production, complex internal geometries, and reduced radar cross-section profiles.

04

Programme goals

Validate and de-risk AM-produced turbojet technology bricks. Deliver a scalable, low-observable propulsion system ready for integration into small-to-medium expendable UAV platforms across European defence fleets.
